Durmitor National Park, Montenegro - Garmin Maps

Posted by us on Sat, Apr 28, 2012
In Garmin, Maps, Summer 2011, Walking
Tags garmin, maps, walking

When we stayed in Durmitor National park, during our August 2011 trip, the guy who seemed to run the camp site at Autocamp Razvršje, gave us a great walking map, which I kept, being a map addict. Actually I think he gave one to everyone, so we weren't special.

Anyway, by luck the other day they sent out a marketing email. Intrigued I browsed their site and noticed they had an electronic copy of the map and others on their site.

They are pretty good maps of the area in 1:25,000 scale. So I thought I would grab a copy for next time I am there. Anyway, follow the link above to their site to get a copy or there are links below.

Also if you need a place to stay, Autocamp Razvršje is as good as any.

Update: Some Garmin devices such as the Montana series allow the importing of custom maps. This is done via Google Earth. You have to import the map as a layer to Google Earth then align it (georeference), then save it as a KMZ. Once this is done you can import it to Garmin's Basecamp or upload it and save it to the Custom Map folder on your GPS.

If you want a copy of the Durmitor KMZ I made click here!
