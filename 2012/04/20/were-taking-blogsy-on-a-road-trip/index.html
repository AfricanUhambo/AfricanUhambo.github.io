We're taking Blogsy on a road trip.

Posted by us on Fri, Apr 20, 2012
In Equipment, General, Overland
Tags blog, review

We have traditionally kept a Moleskin notebook with us when we are on the road, the current one is on its third trip. I would hate for it to go missing.

In the back we record all the cool things like mileages, fuel costs and locations we fill up at. This enables us to keep a log of our fuel consumption. Potentially a sign of an impending problem. We also record accommodation, ferry and toll costs. I often think it would be good if there was more cost information available for overlanders. My intention is to look at the general cost per unit of distance. I believe an accurate assumption can be made for longer trips.

In the front we use it as a diary or journal we record how the day went, what we did, what was said, funny moments and other such things. The journal itself is a good read.

Traditionally when we get home we have every intention of putting our journal story on the web, this often happens many months later. The moment has passed and the story element is lost. Reading the past storys can be a slog. This is for a few reasons; the delay in transferring the story to the site, the size of the task. We are aware of these issues and hate that the moment can be lost in the writing, as the journeys to us are so much more.

To try and improve on these issues we are still going to use our journal, but have decided we will post the story in the style of a blog rather than a webpage. To do this we will use the iPad to post the blog as we go. We often camp and it is at these moments we have time, we are relaxed and in the mood to write these entries. The iPad is the ideal tool for this. The important part of this is we will be immersed in the trip while we are writing, so still living the moment.

To enable us to do this we are going to use a few tools, firstly we will be using Blogsy which is a blogging app for the iPad, I suggest you check it out by the way, it is the best of what I have tried. For some reason this app makes you want to post, it has a great feel and brings together everything you need in a great interface.

We will also be using iPhoto to manage, edit and upload photos. To do this we will be getting a camera connection kit for the iPad shortly. I'll update you on this soon on this.
