When we stayed in Durmitor National park, during our August 2011 trip, the guy who seemed to run the camp site at Autocamp Razvršje, gave us a great walking map, which I kept, being a map addict. Actually I think he gave one to everyone, so we weren't special. Anyway, by luck the other day they sent out a marketing email. DAY 27: THURSDAY 25 AUGUST 2011 Villey le Sec (France) – Hove (England)

August 27, 2011
We woke up in the pouring rain! We decided to pack up and see how the weather goes as we drive. Needless to say, that it did not clear up rain, rain, rain. We stopped just outside reims to get some food from the supermarket. We made the decision that as we were only 200km (give or take), so we decided to just carry on all the way to Calais.

DAY 25/26: 23/24 AUGUST 2011 Altkirch (France) – Villey le Sec (France)

August 25, 2011
Camping Costs: Camping de Villey le Sec €44.60 + electricity – 2 nights A little late rising, but not an issue as we had packed things up the night before. In the morning, tents packed up and off we went back to the supermarket to get a map so we could navigate back to Calais. Map bought, and off we went, in the right direction. We drove to Nancy and decided that we’d pull over and look for a supermarket for food and then a campsite.

DAY 23/24: 21/22 AUGUST 2011 Forno di Zaldo (Italy) – Altkirch (France)

August 23, 2011
Camping Costs: Camping Les Acaises €32 – 2 nights incl. electricity We were up late, but still managed to pack up and leave in good time! It was a long drive today. We managed to find a garage for diesel and luckily some better maps. We started our navigation out of Italy, through Austria, into Switzerland and finally…just through the border into France. Once in France we decided to stop for dinner as it was very late, then go and find a campsite.

DAY 19 - 21: 17 - 21 AUGUST 2011 Trogir (Croatia) – Plitvička Jezera Nationalni Park (Croatia)

August 22, 2011
Camping Costs: Autokamp Korana 171KN per night – 3 nights Well…what a record, up and gone by 8.15hrs!!! Plus, Wendy went to pay for the night and made a complaint about being put on a pitch that wasn’t a pitch but a dog toilet! Well…they didn’t like this, but they still gave us a “discount”. We just wanted to leave this awful place. We decided to head for Plitvička Jezera. No GPS to navigate us, so we decided to just head for the motorway.

DAY 22: SATURDAY 20 AUGUST 2011 Plitvička Jezera (Croatia) – Forno di Zaldo (Italy)

August 21, 2011
Camping Costs: Camping Al Pez €26.80 – 1 night We were up, packed up and gone by 8.30hr! We had decided to miss out Venice as most of the campsites wanted a weeks booking, we only wanted to stay for 2 nights. Instead we were going to head for Northern italy, then using our already purchased Swiss Vignette to bomb through Switzerland and straight through France, using the fast motorways. We didn’t want to get stuck in expensive Austria or Switzerland again.

DAY 18: TUESDAY 16 AUGUST 2011 Lumbarda, Korčula (Croatia) – Trogir (Croatia)

August 17, 2011
Camping Costs: Kamp Seget 191KN – 1 night We managed to leave early today, perhaps that was because we didn’t sleep due to the celebrations that consisted of loud music and singing. We reached Vela Luka at about 9.30hrs, purchased our tickets and went for a wonder around town. We had 4 hours to kill till the ferry. We managed to also find an exchange place that would exchange our Serbian Dinar!

DAY 16/17: 14/15 AUGUST 2011, Mostar (Bosnia) – Lumbarda, Korčula (Croatia)

August 16, 2011
Camping costs: Autokamp Vela Pelarka 99KN per night + 20KN electricity – 2 nights (7KN = €1) Woken by the Call to Prayer, not heard that for years. We went to see the Dervish house in Blagaj before setting off to Croatia. We went down and took a look, but they were busy doing conservation work to the house and rebuilding the surrounding houses around the dervish house. Cassie was amused as a dog followed us from the parking along to the path to the house, and it was waiting for us when we got back.

DAY 15: SATURDAY 13 AUGUST 2011 Donji Morinj (Montenegro) – Mostar (Bosnia)

August 14, 2011
Camping Costs: Sobe (A dodgy private room) €25 – 1 night A fairly early start for us, with not far to travel either. The border crossing was uneventful, even for a Saturday. We managed to make it to Mostar by 14.30hrs. FOOTNOTE: The Garmin screen has given up the ghost. Unable to use it to navigate, we are now subject to map (i.e Wendy) navigation. She seems to get a little better each year though.
