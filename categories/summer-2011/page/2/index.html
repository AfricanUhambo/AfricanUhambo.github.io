African Uhambo

Summer 2011

DAY 13/14: 11/12 AUGUST 2011 Žabljak (Montenegro) – Donji Morinj (Montenegro)

August 13, 2011
Camping Costs: Autokamp Ravela €30 – 2 nights + €6 for electricity Today we drove down to the coast of Montenegro. We first took the scenic route out of Durmitor…what a drive. Amazing views and mountains. We stopped for a coffee along the way and Dan managed to buy himself a Durmitor t-shirt. When we arrived at the coast, and drove around the bay, the views were wonderful. Especially the drive down the mountain to the bay.

DAY 11/12: 9/10 AUGUST 2011 Studenica (Serbia) – Durmitor National Park (Montenegro)

August 11, 2011
Camping Costs: Autokamp Razvršje €10 – 2 nights We attempted to have a lie in this morning, we managed to sleep until 7.50am! A shower to wash away the bed bugs and another cereal breakfast in our room. Off into the Monastery for a look see. The monastery was quiet, we seemed to be the only visitors at that time. The restoration team were there hard at work and inside the chapel it seemed like they had a lot of work on their hands and perhaps for a few many years.

DAY 10: MONDAY 8 AUGUST 2011 Mokra Gora (Serbia) – Studenica (Serbia)

August 09, 2011
Camping costs: Studenica Hotel €15 – 1 night Up early with a breakfast of our cereal and milk. A shower and change and off to Mokra Gora Ŝargan Eight Railway. We got there early, so bought tickets, sorted out the back of the Landy and went to have a coffee. We saw crowds growing and the engine head for the carriages. Dan went to pay and Cassie and Wendy went to get a seat on one of the carriages.

DAY 9: SUNDAY 7 AUGUST 2011 Fruŝka Gora (Serbia) – Mokra Gora (Serbia)

August 08, 2011
Camping Costs: Private room €20 – 1 night We went to see the Ravanica Monastery near Vrdnik. They were having a service, which was expected as it was Sunday. We respected Catholic culture and crossed ourselves as we entered the church. We stayed a few minutes and listened to the priest give his sermon, not that we could understand his Serbian. We left and decided to go to another Monastery. When we got to the next Monastery, there was a sign saying, “No short skirts or strappy tops”.

DAY 8: SATURDAY 6 AUGUST 2012 Velika (Croatia) – Fruŝka Gora (Serbia)

August 07, 2011
Camping Costs: Hotel/Pension Restoran Zmajevac €27 – 1 night We decided to head off into Serbia today but stop in a town called Ilik to see a castle and war memorial. We stopped for coffee which was very nice. Afterwards we headed off to Ilok. The town holds a memorial for the 1991 war. We first stopped at the Ovĉara Memorial which is the site of a mass grave. The towns people of Vukovar were massacred by the Serbs during the invasion.

DAY 7: FRIDAY 5 AUGUST 2012 Dovje (Slovenia) – Velika (Croatia)

August 06, 2011
Camping Costs: Pension Basišiĉ 340.00 KN (Kuna) – 1 night (7KN = €1) As we only had 1 tent to pack up, and we woke up earlier than our alarm, we managed to leave at a fairly decent time today. We didn’t have far to go according to the GPS so we had a nice drive. We had to purchase a Slovenian Vignette for the toll roads and then when we got to Croatia we had our first boarder crossing.

DAY 5/6: WEDNESDAY 3/4 AUGUST 2011, Bürserberg (Austria) – Dovje (Slovenia)

August 05, 2011
Camping Costs: Camp Kamne €50.16 incl. electric – 2 nights Today was a long drive. We set off a little later than we should have and encountered lots of windy mountain passes. We went into Italy with a drastic scenery change, out and back into Austria and then finally into Slovenia. The hairpin bends we drove seemed almost endless. We stopped for diesel and managed to get some bread as well.

DAY4: TUESDAY 2 AUGUST 2012 Stechelberg (Switzerland) – Bürserberg (Austria)

August 03, 2011
Camping costs: Camping Heidi €26.70 – 1 night What a days driving! We left camp later than usual and drove all day but only managed to cover 180 – 200km! We managed to just get inside the Austrian border. We managed to find a supermarket after searching for one for ages and decided to stop to buy as many supllies as we could. Walking around looking for ice tea for Cassie, she slips on the floor and bruised her cox-ix/bottom.

DAY 2/3: SUNDAY 31 July / 1 August 2011 Anould (France) – Stechelberg (Switzerland)

August 02, 2011
Camping Costs: Camping Breithorn 54.80 CHF – 2 nights Nice easy drive today. We decided to stop off in Interlaken for lunch. we managed to find parking and off we went for a wonder. Switzerland is expensive!! We knew this from the start, but this was a little more than we imagined. We finally decided to stop and eat, but at great expense. Wendy’s Aunt, Uncle and Cousins were currently over from New Zealand.

DAY 1: SATURDAY 30 JULY 2011, Laon (France) – Anould (France)

July 31, 2011
Camping Costs: Lѐs Acacias €13.90 – 1 night We thought we’d do a long one today and head for Interlaken, try to get a day ahead. Our start wasn’t brilliant, wrong input into the GPS and after 45min of driving, we realised we were heading back along the way we drove yesterday! After stopping and re-jigging the GPS, lovingly named Hannah (Hannah Montanna, get it?!), we were back on track. But we did have to go back the way we had just come, again!
