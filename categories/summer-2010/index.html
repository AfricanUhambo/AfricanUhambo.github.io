Teplice nad Metuji to Adrspach - Czech Republic

March 19, 2012
While we where in the Czech Republic in August 2010, during our summer holidays we stayed in Teplice Nad Metuji, this is part of a National Park around the Adrspach area, and is a very nice place to visit and walk. The park its self is home to sandstone formations that have been paired with famous monuments and people in name, it covers 17 km square in the Bohemia area of the Northern Czech Republic.

DAY 17 - 19: 23 - 25 AUGUST, Ĉeský Krumlov – Home....

August 26, 2010
Ĉeský Krumlov – Regensburg (Pielenhofen, Germany) Camping costs: €20.70 – 1 night. 50c for shower. Today was the earliest and quickest we have broken camp so far! We were up, packed and on the road by 8.30am! We parked in one of the tourist car parks and walked into town. We thought it was a very pretty place but it must get extremely busy because as we sat down for coffee and cakes, troops of tourists were already walking past us.

DAY 16: 22 AUGUST,Teplice nad Metuji – Ĉeský Krumlov

August 23, 2010
We woke early today. Breaking camp and packing up was easier with our wonderful new tents. Today we are off to Ĉeský Krumlov, stopping along the way at the Church of Bones in Kostince near Kutná Hora. We were traffic lucky today and made it to Kostnice in good time. The church is the outcome of an estimated 40 000 to 70 000 peoples bones used to decorate the church. The church is said to be a reminder of our humanity, that without redemption we cannot enter heaven.

DAY 14/15: 20/21 AUGUST Martin (Slovakia) – Teplice nad Metuji (Czech Republic)

August 22, 2010
Camping costs: 560 Crowns – 2 nights = €23.00 After an initial hiccup of taking the wrong exit and having to do a big loop, we finally made our way to the Czech. Unfortunately for us the road was VERY slow going. Not a motorway, still a major road, but FULL of trucks and slow moving vehicles making it a long journey. We stopped at 12pm for lunch and a drink, but we were still stuck at about 2pm in a traffic jam with still roughly 90km to go till we get to Adršpach.

DAY 13: 19 AUGUST, Vodńa Nádž (Voklav, Slovakia) – Martin (Slovakia)

August 20, 2010
Hotel cost: €80 double room Up at the normal time, packed up and made tracks for Martin. Not too much of a journey today thankfully. We reached Martin at about 2pm and parked up to look for a hotel/B&B. We managed to find a B&B, but it happened to be a 4* hotel. A decision was made to stay for the night. For a 4* hotel, the room were pretty undistinguishable and there was no air conditioning.

DAY 11/12: 17/18 AUGUST, Nová Sedlica – Vodńa Nádž (Voklav, Slovakia)

August 19, 2010
Camping costs: €22 – 2 nights for 3 adults, 1 child, 2 tents & 2 cars We decided to stop at the pension we had wanted to stay at for a drink before setting off again. What a pity it had been full, it looked like a lovely place to stay. Perhaps if we are back in that neck of the woods again we might book into it. Out of interest we asked when they would have a free room, they said they were fully booked for the next week.

DAY 10: 16 AUGUST, Poroszlo (Hungary) – Nová Sedlica (Slovakia)

August 17, 2010
Pension: €10.00 pppn €5.00 for a child Up bright and early, packed up and ready to leave at our best time yet! Off into Slovakia! Tokaj (pronounced Tokay), is a lovely little town with lovely cheap wine. We bought 3 bottles and a grape juice or non alcoholic wine. The bottles were 1.5ℓ as well. Ready to head off out into Slovakia. The roads were awful. HUGE potholes, making you drive in the centre of the road so as to avoid them.

DAY 9: 15 AUGUST, Keszthely (Hungary) – Lake Tisza (Poroszlo, Hungary)

August 16, 2010
Not a long journey today distance wise, but a long journey due to road conditions and traffic. A very muggy day today. Cassie and Dan both sat in the car topless. We managed to successfully navigate our way through Budapest, but only after getting lost trying to find the M3 exit. Oh well…you can’t have it all. Since Bovec, we have experienced 4 thunderstorms. This time we drove straight into one.

DAY 7/8: 13/14 AUGUST, Bovec (Slovenia) – Lake Balaton (Keszthely, Hungary)

August 15, 2010
Camping costs: 13,264.70 Fontir = €47.20 (281 Fontir = €1) – 2 nights The journey this morning took us up a mountain pass, over and down again. Half way up the pass, Cas’ tummy couldn’t take the windy road and she emptied her breakfast all over the back seat. We had to pull over and clean up. Just as we started to clean up, the heavens opened and Dan and I got soaked!

DAY 5/6: 11/12 AUGUST, Altenmarkt (Austria) – Bovec (Slovenia)

August 13, 2010
Camping costs: €38.04 – 2 nights Another wet start to the day and packing up a wet tent. Last night we had discussed a few nights stop at Bovec and then deciding where to go from there. We were now sick of rain and thunderstorms as that is all we had had every evening. We were wondering about changing direction and going South to Bosnia and Croatia. We were still undecided as to which direction to go and decided to make our decision in Bovec.
