Day 3 - Neufchateau to Westerheim

July 29, 2013
We started the day with a good deed, jump starting a fellow camper who had apparently defective heater plugs and a weak battery. I did not ask I just obliged. Killing miles again today, we made a plan to be beyond Stuttgart before we stopped. We were hanging by the time we got to Stuttgart, so as we passed we looked up a campsite on the GPS. We picked the nearest in the direction we were heading.

Day 2 - Neufchateau to Weimheim

July 28, 2013
We decided that Cassie was to sleep in her own tent last night. We set her up and put her to bed. Dan and Wendy enjoyed another beer and Dan went off for a shower. While Dan was on his way back from the shower, Cas got up for a wee, but only for Wendy to grab her and hold her as a LARGE GALE swept across the area. Dan mentioned branches falling as he walked back to camp, meanwhile Cassie and Wendy were holding down the ladder in hope the Landy and Tent would not blow over sideways!

Day 1 - Licques to Nuefchateau

July 27, 2013
So after the shenanigans at the Eurotunnel we arrived at Calais at jut gone nine. Seemed a bit late to be looking for a campsite. We were off the train in little time, thanks to the efficient loading and unloading arrangement. We head straight for Reims. The plan at this stage was to just find a place to bed down for the night, so after ten minutes or so on the road, I managed to persuade the GPS to take us via a campsite.

Almost gone

July 26, 2013
Well we are at this time just over 40 minutes late on our booked departure time, the board is saying we'll depart an hour and six minutes late. Luckily apart from not having a place to stay yet when we get to France, we have nowhere important to be. Seen a nicely refurbished Mercedes truck, which has been converted for overland travel, it is from Switerland. I remember seeing those things as a kid in the desert with water tanks on the back tackling dunes.

Almost ready, but where are we going? Can you help?

July 25, 2013
So tomorrow we depart for our next trip. Yet again I am nervous, this time we are once again unaccompanied, and heading to new territory. Why do people keep asking if we will be safe? We have spent the last two evenings getting stuff together, packing the boxes and making sure we are organised. What have we forgotten? I would say we have about two thirds of the kit in the Land Rover, so that after work tomorrow we can chuck in the last few items and hit the road.

Upriser/Upstand in

July 20, 2013
Not quite as far as I would have liked today. Missed the nut and bolt store. They close at 1400 on a Saturday. I managed to get the Upstand and floor fitted and the two central seat brackets. Tomorrow when my drill is charged again, I'll get ready for the last two seat brackets, when I get the bolts on Monday they'll be fitted. I should also be able to refit the sound proofing tomorrow, that is the rubber chequer plate stuff from write off road.

Almost There Upstand Assembly

July 19, 2013
Not much of an update tonight, with the ongoing repair apart from to show some pictures of the panel now it has been fully tweaked and drilled to fit. I finished of the drilling tonight and deburred everything. Final job for the night was to give another coat of etch primer and paint the seat brackets and new panels with stone chip paint. They are now drying, so tomorrow I'll start fitting it for real.

Starting the Upstand/Upriser Re-Assembly

July 18, 2013
I am now at the stage of re-assembly. It has been a slow road even to strip the back down. The new mid-crossmember is galvanised and from YRM metal solutions. One of the nice features of the new one, is that the top brackets are loose rather than welded on this makes it easier to get the cross member under the fuel pipes etc. The only problem I have had with the crossmember is that the holes, for mounting to the chassis rails where about 3mm out across the vehicle.
